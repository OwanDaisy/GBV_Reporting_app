Breaking the Cycle

Shared by Amina, 29 â€¢ Survivor of forced marriage and honor-based violence

In my community, women's choices were never their own. At 19, my family arranged my marriage to a man twice my age. I was told it was for my protection and our family's honor, but I soon discovered it was a prison sentence disguised as tradition.

For years, I endured emotional abuse and control under the guise of cultural norms. I wasn't allowed to pursue education, have friends, or even choose what to wear. When I expressed my desire to study nursing, I was told I was bringing shame to the family by being "too ambitious."

The turning point came when my younger sister turned 17, and my family began arranging her marriage. Seeing history repeat itself gave me courage I didn't know I had. I reached out to a university professor I had secretly been in contact with, who connected me with an organization that helps women in forced marriages.

Leaving was the most difficult decision of my life. I knew I would lose my family and community, but I couldn't bear to see my sister suffer the same fate. With the help of advocates, I found safe housing and legal support to nullify my marriage.

Today, I'm a registered nurse and advocate for women's rights in traditional communities. My sister is now in university studying law, and we've built a new chosen family of supportive friends and mentors.

Culture should never be a weapon used against women. True honor lies in respecting individual dignity and choice. To anyone facing similar circumstances: Your dreams matter, your voice matters, and there are people who can help you find safety while respecting your cultural identity.
